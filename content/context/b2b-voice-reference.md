# B2B voice reference: leasey.ai/blog (operator-facing)

Read from www.leasey.ai/blog on 2026-06-11. This is the ACTIVE voice guide for the
operator-facing channels: blog-writer, press-release, and linkedin-company.
(Not for renter-facing content; that voice lives in blog-voice-reference.md.)

## Two eras on the blog: copy the current one
Older posts use playful clickbait ("Unleash Your Inner Wizard", "Here's the Tea").
DO NOT copy that era. The current B2B era is professional, benefit-focused and
educational, with the product woven in organically. That is the voice to replicate.

## Voice characteristics (verbatim-grounded)
- Second person throughout: speaks to "you", the property manager or landlord.
  Ex: "Whether you manage..." / "Still posting Facebook Marketplace listings one by one?"
- Problem-solution framing from the first lines. Open with the operator's pain,
  then pivot to what solves it. Ex: "Property management software addresses the
  core challenges of rental operations by automating tenant screening, leasing
  workflows, rent collection, and compliance tracking."
- Professional but approachable. One light, relatable image is fine
  ("finding the right qualified tenants can often feel like searching for a
  needle in a haystack"), never sustained humor or pop-culture voice.
- Confidence-building without hyperbole. Benefit-focused, concrete, no hype words.
- Positions automation as freeing the manager to "focus on growing their
  business", never as replacing human judgment.
- Sentence rhythm: short imperatives mixed with longer explanatory sentences.

## Structure (copy this)
- Hook: a question or pain statement in the first 1-2 lines, sometimes followed
  by 3 bullet-pointed benefits before the body.
- Descriptive, action-oriented H2s per benefit/topic; H3s for feature detail.
- Heavy use of numbered steps ("7-step listing process"), numbered tips, and
  comparison tables. Highly scannable.
- Leasey woven in organically: introduced early as the solution provider, then
  featured in the automation sections as the efficient alternative to manual
  work. Educational first, sales subtle.
- Internal links embedded throughout (features, case studies, resources).
- Dedicated closing section that names Leasey (e.g. "Master X With Leasey.AI")
  listing core capabilities, then a direct CTA.

## CTA phrasing (verbatim from the site)
- "Schedule a call with Leasey.AI to get started today and see how it can
  transform your leasing process."
- "...visit our website to set up a call with our team."
The system CTA target stays https://www.leasey.ai/get-started/ . "Schedule a
call" / "set up a call with our team" is the house phrasing for the demo CTA.

## Per channel
- Blog (blog-writer): full pattern above. Question/pain hook, scannable H2/H3,
  numbered lists, Leasey woven in, dedicated closing section + schedule-a-call CTA.
- Press release (press-release): same professional register, drop the second
  person in body copy (press is third person), keep concrete benefit language
  and zero hyperbole. Quotes can carry the warmth.
- LinkedIn company (linkedin-company): the same voice compressed. Pain/question
  opener, benefit-concrete middle, schedule-a-call-flavored soft close.

## System rules still apply on top
English. No em-dashes (the site sometimes uses them in tables; we do not).
Insight-led with named sources. Real client anchors. Demo CTA per channel rules.
