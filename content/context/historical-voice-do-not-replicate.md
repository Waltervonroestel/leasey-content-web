# Historical voice — DO NOT replicate

Source: Brand Voice Guidelines + Leasing Mastery Series, from En Algun Lugar Estudio (former agency, circa 2023). These are valuable to know about but are **not the current voice**. The June 2026 onboarding guide explicitly directs to NOT copy the playful 2023 era of the blog.

## What's deprecated (do NOT replicate)
- "Goofy" / "meme-friendly" tone with phrases like "my dear trendsetter," "strut in looking flawless," "kick-butt property listing," "Hurrah!", "shower your prospects with options."
- Clickbait-style listicle titles: "Unleash Your Inner Wizard," "Tea on Student Rentals," "Tech-Tastic Tricks."
- Audience profile in the old voice doc: "Male, 40+, not tech savvy, lazy, disorganized, resistant to change, managing 100-300 properties." This was the SMB landlord ICP of 2023, **not** Leasey's current ICP (multifamily 100+ doors, SFR/BTR 100+ homes, institutional).
- Casual asides like "Sorry, did we say pouring? We meant flooding."
- The "I" pronoun for author-signed blogs (current site uses "we" / company voice).

## What IS still valid (carry forward)
- **British English** is the house standard (when in doubt, use British). E.g., "neighbourhood," "organised," "centralise."
- **Oxford comma** is required.
- After a colon (except before a proper noun), use a lowercase letter.
- **Brand stylization: "Leasey.AI"** (capital L, lowercase eas, capital AI). Not "leasey.ai" in body copy (that's only the domain).
- LinkedIn from the company page uses "we."
- The 70% manual-effort-reduction figure originated here and is still a current claim (confirmed in the onboarding guide one-liner: "fill vacancies up to 60% faster with 70% less manual effort").
- The internal CTA pattern "Sign up for a Leasey.AI demo" → "let our Client Success Manager onboard your properties" maps to the current "Schedule a call" → onboarding sessions language.

## A note about em-dashes
The historical guidelines say "use em-dashes with a space on each side." Walter explicitly decided **the system does not use em-dashes** in new content (use commas, parentheses, or periods). Walter wins. If you see em-dashes in the live site, that's the historical guideline showing through; new generation does not produce them.

## Why this file exists
So writers know the playful 2023 voice is historical, not a target. If a future operator-facing prompt drifts toward "kick-butt" or "Hurrah!", it's a sign the agent picked up the wrong reference. Re-anchor to `context/b2b-voice-reference.md` (the current leasey.ai/blog voice).
