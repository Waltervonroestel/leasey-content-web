# Positioning and supporting data (source of truth)

Leasey content is INSIGHT-LED: open with a real datapoint or insight, use it to frame an operator problem, then connect to the matching positioning pillar. Never the other way around (do not start with the product and force the data). The double goal: deliver real knowledge AND strengthen Leasey's positioning as the authority in leasing automation.

Rules:
- Every piece carries at least ONE verifiable datapoint or insight (from this file, `context/signals.md` or fresh research). With a source.
- The datapoint must connect to a pillar below, not be decorative.
- Never invent numbers. If there is no support, `[VERIFY]` or ask news-researcher for research.
- Refresh data with `news-researcher` when it ages (market numbers are Q1 2026).
- ATTRIBUTION: name the source in the text (e.g. "according to Yardi's Q1 2026 report"). Leasey's own product numbers are self-sourced.

## The 5 positioning pillars

### Pillar 1: Speed wins in a cooling market
- Position: in a soft market, response speed is the only lever the operator controls. Leasey maximizes it.
- Supporting data:
  - Canada national vacancy 5.1% in Q1 2026, ninth straight quarterly rise; Calgary 7.3%, Edmonton 6.2% (Yardi Q1 2026). https://www.yardi.com/news/press-releases/rent-growth-hits-4-year-low-in-q1-2026/
  - Annual rent growth 2.7% (4-year low); lease-over-lease on new leases -1.0% nationally.
  - 70% of prospects search after hours (ShowMojo). A lead not answered at night goes cold.
  - Typical manual response rate 30-40% vs 100% automated (products.md).
- Leasey connection: Liza 24/7, instant response, fills vacancies up to 60% faster.

### Pillar 3: All-in-one, from listing to signature
- Position: competitors cover one piece (scheduling, or screening, or CRM). Leasey covers syndication to signature in one layer.
- Supporting data:
  - Syndication to 48+ marketplaces (products.md). Scheduling competitors do not syndicate or do screening/documents.
  - 30-50% of proptech VC now goes to AI (vs ~20% last year): the market fragments into many point tools (Commercial Observer / industry).
  - The #1 adoption barrier is integration with existing systems, above cost, ROI and training (signal N6, Frontdesk/Propmodo 2026). Operators do not want to switch PMS. https://propmodo.com/what-the-data-shows-about-the-biggest-trends-in-multifamily-property-management/
- Leasey connection: a stack from listing to native eSignature, automates ~90% of leasing tasks. And it integrates ON TOP of the existing PMS (Yardi two-way sync, Buildium, Rent Manager), implemented in a day. The market's #1 barrier is exactly Leasey's differentiator.

### Pillar 4: Canadian-first
- Position: most tools are US-first and treat Canada as a footnote. Leasey is built for Canadian regulation, platforms and screening.
- Supporting data:
  - Canadian market with its own dynamic (very uneven provincial vacancy: Calgary 7.3% vs Halifax 2.8%, Yardi Q1 2026).
  - Canadian integrations: SingleKey (screening), Kijiji, Duuo by Co-operators (insurance).
  - Fair Housing and multi-jurisdiction regulation: real CA/US complexity (Multifamily Dive, 2026). https://www.multifamilydive.com/news/ai-leasing-tools-hidden-legal-risk-ada-compliance/817626/
- Leasey connection: native Canadian platforms and screening, plus US coverage.

## How to use this when writing
1. Pick the post angle and the pillar it fits.
2. Open with the datapoint/insight that frames the problem (not with Leasey), NAMING the source in the text (e.g. "according to Yardi's Q1 2026 Multifamily Report"). Blog can link; LinkedIn/Reddit names it.
3. Develop the problem from the operator's reality (clients.md).
4. Connect to the pillar and the Leasey feature that solves it (products.md). Leasey's own numbers are self-sourced.
5. Close with the channel CTA (operator: demo; renter: soft touch).

Attribution note: the source in the text must be nameable and credible (Yardi, RBC, Frontdesk Research, StatCan, Propmodo, etc.), not a vague "studies show". If the data comes from signals.md, use the source named there.
